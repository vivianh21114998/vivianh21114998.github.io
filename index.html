Introduction and Early History

Founded in 1995 in Los Angeles, Lifehouse is an alternative rock band (some consider them to be Christian). The original band name was called Blyss from 1995-2000.
The members consist of Jason Wade as the lead singer and guitarist, Rick Woolstenhulme Jr. as the drummer, and Bryce Soderberg as the bassist. They were most famous
during the 2000s, but they are still creating music. As of 2018, their most recent released album was called Out of the Wastelands.
Their first successful album was No Name Face in 2000. The band's first song to reach the Billboard chart was "Hanging by a Moment," which was the third
song in chart history to be named the "No.1 Song of the Year."
